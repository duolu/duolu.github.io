Establishing A Hand-Gesture Research Platform

for Behavior Biometrics and Cognitive Robotics (HGRP)

Hand-gesture based research has broad and profound impact on addressing our nation's priorities and societal needs, e.g., manipulators and co-robot in manufacturing, natural HCI for virtual reality, wearable platforms in consumer electronics, hand gesture biometrics in cybersecurity, etc.  However, identifying and recognizing hand gestures are extremely challenging for computer vision research due to the lack of discriminative features and the deformable nature of hands. To overcome this challenge, in this project, we propose to establish a hand-gesture focused computing research infrastructure, called HGRP (i.e., Hand-Gesture Research Platform) at Arizona State University, to allow researchers easily accessing various hand-gesture data to validate their hand-gesture recognition models, benchmark the performance of newly developed algorithms, and compare with research outcomes from others.  

The objective of HGRP is threefold:

  1. To establish a large-scale openly available dataset on hand gesture.
  2. To develop tools and maintain a codebase for hand gesture data processing.
  3. To provide cloud resources as well as online services hosted by a cloud platform providing GPU-capable computation and storage for hand-gesture related research.

With HGRP, researchers will focus on developing new algorithms and conducting performance evaluation without putting significant efforts for data collection, cleansing, and purchasing expensive GPU hours.

HGRP is currently supported by NSF-CCRI-CNS-1925709.
